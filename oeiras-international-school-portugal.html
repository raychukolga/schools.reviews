Oeiras International School is one of Portugal's most academically outstanding international schools — and one of its most unusual. It is entirely non-profit, governed and run by a charitable foundation of parents, with every euro reinvested back into the school. There are no shareholders, no commercial operators. The community owns it.

The school offers the full IB continuum — PYP, MYP and Diploma — making it one of only three international schools in Portugal to do so. Class sizes are deliberately small: the maximum is 18 students, with an average of 16. That ratio is rare in the Lisbon area.

The school operates across three campuses, all close to one another in Oeiras. The historic main campus sits within a quinta — a traditional Portuguese estate — with a river running through the grounds and a forest alongside. It is genuinely beautiful. A brand-new senior campus is opening in September 2026. Sport, music, and a wide range of activities are woven into the programme, with football, swimming, and basketball included in the regular schedule alongside a skate park and large playground on campus.

Frequently asked questions
Is Oeiras International School a good school academically?
Yes — it is among the top academic performers in Portugal and Europe. The school's IB Diploma average score is 36 out of 45, placing it in the top 10 in Europe. The world average is 30.2. The school publishes its results transparently.
Is Oeiras International School non-profit?
Yes — it is fully non-profit and governed by a charitable parent foundation. There are no shareholders or commercial operators. All surplus fees are reinvested directly into the school. This is unusual among international schools in Portugal.
How much does Oeiras International School cost per year?
Annual tuition ranges from €13,980 (Grade 1–3) to €22,406 (Grade 12–13). There is a one-time enrollment fee of €5,000 and an application fee of €250. Always verify current fees directly with the school.
Is there a waiting list?
Yes — Oeiras International School is very popular and typically operates with a waiting list. Families are strongly advised to apply as early as possible, even before relocating to Portugal.
What curriculum does Oeiras International School follow?
The school follows the full International Baccalaureate continuum — Primary Years Programme (PYP), Middle Years Programme (MYP), and IB Diploma (DP). It is one of only three schools in Portugal to offer all three IB programmes in sequence. For a comparison of IB with other curricula, see our IB vs Cambridge guide.
How many students are in each class?
Class sizes are small — a maximum of 18 students and an average of 16. This is significantly below most international schools in the Lisbon area, where class sizes of 20–25 are more common. For comparison, ISS Taguspark has classes of up to 25 students.
