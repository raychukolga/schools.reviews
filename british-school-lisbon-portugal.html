🏫 BSL has 2 campuses in Lisbon

The British School of Lisbon operates two campuses: Cais do Sodré (Nursery–Year 5) in central Lisbon, and the new Restelo campus (Year 6+), opened September 2024 within the historic Os Belenenses sports complex. Students transition to Restelo in Year 6.

Cais do Sodré · Nursery–Year 5 Restelo · Year 6+ (opened 2024)
🌍 Part of Dukes Education — global network

BSL joined Dukes Education in January 2026. Dukes operates schools across the UK, Croatia, Czechia, Greece, Ireland, Poland, Romania, Spain and Switzerland — making BSL their fifth school in Portugal. This brings access to a global talent pool of educators, student exchanges, and shared best practice across the network.

BSL has 46 nationalities in its community and maintains class sizes of 18–22 students. It is a COBIS member and recognised by the República Portuguesa. The Restelo campus, located within the Os Belenenses sports complex, gives students access to top-level sporting facilities and connects them to one of Lisbon's most beloved football clubs — reflecting BSL's philosophy of educating the whole child academically, socially and physically.

Frequently asked questions
What are the two BSL campuses and how do they work?
BSL operates two campuses in Lisbon. The original Cais do Sodré campus (Rua de S. Paulo 89) serves Nursery through Year 5 and is located in one of central Lisbon's most accessible areas. The new Restelo campus (Rua de Alcolena 10), which opened in September 2024, serves Year 6 and above and is situated within the Os Belenenses sports complex — 12 minutes from Cais do Sodré. Students transition to Restelo in Year 6 as part of the school's middle school model.
What curriculum does BSL follow, and when will IGCSE and A Levels be available?
BSL follows the English National Curriculum — the same framework used by the UK's leading independent schools — adapted to include Portuguese language and culture. Students in Years 10–11 prepare for IGCSE examinations. Year 11 begins in September 2026, and a full AS Level and A Level programme launches in September 2027. Families with children currently in Year 7–9 should factor this timeline into their planning.
What is Dukes Education and what does it mean for BSL?
Dukes Education is a global group of schools operating in the UK, Croatia, Czechia, Greece, Ireland, Poland, Romania, Spain and Switzerland. BSL joined the Dukes family in January 2026, becoming their fifth school in Portugal. The partnership gives BSL access to a global talent pool of educators, international student exchange programmes, shared curriculum resources, and the collective experience of a well-established education group. For families, it means the school has institutional backing and a longer-term development plan.
What are the first-year costs at BSL?
For a child starting in Year 1, first-year costs include: tuition (€15,818) + one-time enrolment fee (€5,000 per family) + application fee (€250) + lunch if required (€1,800/yr) = approximately €22,868 in year one. The enrolment fee is a one-time payment per family — it does not apply again for subsequent children or years. Transport and uniform are additional.
Is BSL a good choice for families new to Lisbon?
BSL works well for families coming from British schools or those planning to return to the UK system — the English National Curriculum and IGCSE pathway are familiar and transferable. The central Cais do Sodré location makes it easy to access from most parts of Lisbon. The school is still growing (founded 2019), which means families become part of a school building its community and identity. For older students specifically heading for A Levels, it is worth confirming the exact timeline for Year 11 and 12 with the admissions team.
